Location and Surrounding Languages

Abesabesi is spoken in the North of Ondo State in Nigeria. Of the seven towns where Abesabesi is spoken, one - Akunnu - is situateded in the Akoko North East LGA (Local Government Area). The other towns are within the Akoko North West LGA. These are Ibaram, Iyani, Ikaram, Ase, Gedegede and Ajowa. Ajowa is a rather recent settlement, which is composed of seven communities that have moved there mostly due to disputes. Of these seven communities (Uro, Daja, Ojo, Efifa, Iludun, Eshuku, Ora), three communities speak Abesabesi (Daja, Eshuku, Iludun), two communities speak Akokoid (Uro, Ojo) and the remaining two speak the local Yoruba variety Owe (Efifa, Ora). The Efifa community originally spoke Abesabesi but switched to Owe (ELABORATE).
